Brené Brown

The power of vulnerability

"Those who have a strong sense of love and belonging have the corauge to be imperfect." - Brené Brown

Biography

Brené Brown is a research professor at the University of Houston Graduate College of Social Work. She has spent the past ten years studying vulnerability, courage, authenticity, and shame. She spent the first five years of her decade-long study focusing on shame and empathy, and is now using that work to explore a concept that she calls Wholeheartedness. She poses the questions: How do we learn to embrace our vulnerabilities and imperfections so that we can engage in our lives from a place of authenticity and worthiness? How do we cultivate the courage, compassion, and connection that we need to recognize that we are enough – that we are worthy of love, belonging, and joy?

Short timeline of Brown's life

  • 18 November 1965 Brown was born in San Antonio(Texas)
  • 1995, she completed her Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) at the University of Texas at Austin
  • 1996 Master of Social Work (MSW)
  • 2002 PhD, Social Work, University of Houston Graduate College of Social Work
  • 2009, Houston Woman Magazine voted Brown one of Houston's most influential women of the year
  • 2010 Her TED Talk in June is one of the most watched talks on the Ted.com website
  • 2016, the Huffington Foundation honored Brown by pledging $2 million over four years to fund the Brené Brown Endowed Chair in the Graduate College of Social Work at the University of Houston.
